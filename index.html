    • Puerto Rico

    Description and Geography


    Capital:

    San Juan


    Official Languages:

    Spanish and English


    Location:

    Caribbean, island between the Caribbean Sea and the North Atlantic Ocean, east of the Dominican Republic.


    Climate:

    The climate is Tropical Marine with average temperatures year round, near 80 °F (26.7 °C) in lower elevations and 70 °F (21.1 °C) in the mountains.


    National Holiday:

    November 19 (The Discovery of Puerto Rico date).

    National Hymn (Anthem): "La Borinqueña"

    Total Area: 9,104 sq km (3,508 sq mi)


    Limits:

    To the west by Haití and the Dominican Republic, separated by the Mona Passage, to the east by the Virgin Islands, to the north by the Atlantic Ocean, and to the south by the Caribbean Sea.


    Borders:

    Puerto Rico is under the U.S. customs jurisdiction. Borders are open between P.R. and the U.S., allowing for free movement of people and merchandise.


    Maritime claims:

    Exclusive economic zone: 200 NM

    Territorial sea:12 NM


    Hurricane Maria

           On August 22nd 2018, Hurrican Maria wreaked havoc on the island of Puerto Rico. Winds up to 155 miles per hour tore apart 300 mile island. While other countries were also badly hurt from the damage of the storm, Puerto Rico embraced the full power of the storm. It was one of the worst storms to hit the island in over 50 years.

           Today, the island has a rough estimate of 60% power. This number rapidly flucuates due to the electrical grid being drastically delicate. Puerto Rico has not been able to get back on its feet since that Summer day and has been heavily dependent on donations to survive. Over 300 deaths have been recorded since the hurricane due to the effects of the storm. Hospitals didn't have the necessary resources to treat patience and electricity was inconsistent. The Carribbean island needs 90% of its resources imported from other countries, even the motherland United States. Mass amount of donations ranging from food, water, clothes, batteries, and other resources were charitably donated territory in a time of need.
